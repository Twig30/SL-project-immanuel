Environmental issues

One main Environmental issue that the amazon rainforest today is Deforestation, which is fueled by logging, infrastructural development, and agriculture, is the main cause of the serious environmental problems the Amazon rainforest faces. In addition to endangering biodiversity, this damage releases enormous amounts of carbon dioxide that are stored in trees, which in turn alters the global climate. In addition, pollution and illicit mining worsen ecosystems, endangering not only the complex web of life that depends on this essential habitat but also the livelihoods of Indigenous groups.

Causes to these issues

The main causes of deforestation in the Amazon rainforest are agricultural practices, particularly cattle ranching and the production of cash crops like soy, which require large-scale land clearing. A major factor is also played by logging, both legal and illicit, since trees are taken down for their wood and paper goods. Road construction and urbanization are examples of infrastructure development that contributes to deforestation by increasing accessibility to and vulnerability to exploitation of isolated areas.
